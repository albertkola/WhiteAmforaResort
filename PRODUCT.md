# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International and Albanian travelers planning a summer stay on the northern Adriatic
coast (Shëngjin / Rana e Hedhun). Primarily **families, couples, and small groups** who
want **spacious, self-catering apartments** — kitchens, living rooms, sea-view balconies —
rather than a standard hotel room. They arrive mostly on **mobile**, often mid-trip-planning,
comparing a handful of properties. Two audiences in equal measure: **English** speakers
(foreign visitors) and **Albanian** speakers (domestic / diaspora). Their job: quickly judge
whether this is the right beachfront base, then reach the owner to ask about dates and book.

## Product Purpose

A marketing site that presents White Amfora's apartments and its location, and converts
interest into **direct inquiries**. There is **no in-house booking engine**: the site routes
guests to WhatsApp, email, and the Booking.com listing. Success = a guest understands the
offer (sea-view self-catering apartments by a quiet, scenic beach), trusts it, and sends an
inquiry or opens Booking.com with minimal friction, in their own language.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, refined, coastal.** A serene Adriatic escape, not a busy resort. Voice is warm,
unhurried, and specific — sensory detail about the place (the dune beach, the balcony, the
light) over adjectives and claims. It should feel like a quiet recommendation from someone
with good taste. Emotional goal: calm, anticipation, trust.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate chain hotel sites** — sterile, templated, stocky, predictable. White Amfora is
  small and particular; it should feel personal, not institutional.
- **Party / club beach resorts** — neon, nightlife energy, promo-banner clutter. Wrong mood
  entirely; this is a restful escape.
- **Generic booking-listing density** — the cramped, info-dense, photo-grid Booking.com /
  Airbnb feel. We present, we don't list.
- **Identity guard:** never drift toward the unrelated *Amfora Hotel & Spa, Durrës*. This is
  *White Amfora*, Shëngjin.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the place speak.** Real photography and concrete location detail (the beach, the
   balcony view, the distances) carry the site — not adjectives or stock luxury tropes.
2. **Honest by default.** Show only confirmed amenities and real specifics. No invented
   pools, awards, or services. Unconfirmed items wait for client confirmation.
3. **Inquiry over friction.** Reaching the owner is the core action: make WhatsApp / email /
   Booking always one tap away, with prefilled, natural messages, in the active language.
4. **Quiet luxury.** Restraint, generous whitespace, and one committed direction over
   decoration. Calm beats loud; nothing templatey or cluttered.
5. **Bilingual parity.** English and Albanian are first-class equals — same polish, same copy
   quality, every string from locale files.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Semantic HTML, full keyboard navigation (nav, mobile drawer, forms,
gallery lightbox), visible focus states, alt text on all meaningful imagery, color contrast
AA across the palette, and a genuine `prefers-reduced-motion` path (motion fully disabled, no
content gated behind reveal transitions). Bilingual EN/SQ with correct `lang`/`hreflang`.
